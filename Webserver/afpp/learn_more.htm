  • Peptides play important roles in signalling, regulation and immunity within an organism. Many have successfully been used as therapeutic products often mimicking naturally occurring peptides. New antibiotic drugs are needed urgently to address the problem of bacterial resistance and bioactive peptides may provide an answer. However, bioactive peptides are not only important as a potential source of new antibiotic drugs but have also been shown to have a potential role in the development of new antiviral, antifungal and antiparasitic drugs that may be less susceptible to the development of resistance in pathogens. Additionally, anti-cancer peptides have recently emerged as promising therapeutic agents for cancer treatment. With bioactive peptides showing such potential as new therapeutics, nutraceuticals and functional food ingredients, the discovery and prediction of new bioactive peptides is an increasingly valuable research area.

  • AFP is a broad type of defensin produced by some Ascomycetes, mainly from Aspergillus and Penicillium. AFP is a small (about 50 amino acid residues), secreted, amphiphilic, cationic and cysteine-rich protein (CRP) containing six to eight cysteine residues and folded into A tight disulfide-stabilized structure with five Î²-strands provides high stability.Significant diversity of AFP-like genes and proteins is widespread in Ascomycetes, whose genome encodes up to three different sequence-related AFPs, providing a rich source of potentially different antifungal agents. We also need to understand the structure and mode of action of AFP in order to be used as an antifungal compound in the future. To date, we have identified antifungal peptides in a variety of life forms, including plants, mammals and microorganisms. The structure of antifungal peptides is diverse, and some semi-synthetic and fully synthetic analogs have been developed. Synthetic peptides have some functions superior to natural peptides, have broad prospects for combating fungal infections, and have entered Clinical trial phase.
